Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

by Jonathan Hinkey

Posted on August 29, 2014 at 11:00 AM CST

A time in my life when I felt the happiest, the proudest, and the most satisfied.

A time in my life when I felt the happiest, the proudest, and the most satisfied was when I was about 10 years old. The summer after 5th grade my friends and I spent most of the summer building a fort in the woods by my house. The values that come to mind when I think of this experience are: accomplishment, achievement, adventure, ambition, close relationships, community, confidence, freedom, friendship, independence, leadership, and meaningful work. I think this was such a great experience because it was the first time in my life where my friends and I felt truly independent. We were brought together by our, project and we worked hard that summer on our fort. Without any parental supervision or any tools, other than a hatchet, we built a solid fort with a roof made out of bark and walls made of sticks.

I think independence was the best of all the values listed above. It was the first time in my life I made something that I was proud of, without the help of an adult. I still try to live up to these values. In fact this experience of building, coupled with a few more later in life, were the reasons I got into construction.

When I think about these values I feel a sense of pride. If I think to myself "How would I feel if someone was threatening my values?" that would make me more sensative to other's feelings in the group.

Other exercises I can use to help myself when I am feeling down at DBC are reflecting on other fond memories and taking a moment to reflect on the situation and to remember to take it one step at a time.
